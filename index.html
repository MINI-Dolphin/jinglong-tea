Nestled in the ancient mountains of Yunnan Province, JingLong Tea Factory has been crafting premium Pu-erh tea for over three decades. Our journey began with a single ancient tea garden and a deep reverence for the art of tea-making.

The Art of Brewing

Brew Like a Master

Pu-erh rewards patience and attention. Follow these steps to unlock the full complexity of your tea.

💧
01

Rinse the Cake

· 醒茶

Break off 5-8g of tea. Rinse briefly with boiling water (95-100°C) for 5 seconds to awaken the leaves. Discard this first rinse.

🫖
02

First Steep

· 头泡

Pour boiling water in a circular motion. Steep for 20-30 seconds. The liquor should be a clear amber or deep red.

🍵
03

Pour & Appreciate

· 品茗

Pour through a strainer into a fairness cup, then into small tasting cups. Observe the color, inhale the aroma.

♾️
04

Multiple Steepings

· 多泡

Quality Pu-erh can be steeped 10-20 times. Gradually increase steeping time with each infusion to explore evolving flavors.

✦ Pro Tips

  • →Use 100°C water for aged Pu-erh; 90-95°C for young raw teas
  • →A Yixing clay teapot enhances the earthy notes of ripe Pu-erh
  • →Store your tea cake in a cool, ventilated space away from odors
  • →The best Pu-erh improves with every year — don't rush to drink it all
